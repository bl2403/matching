Instructions

In this part of the experiment, you will be asked to provide advice to several subjects of other preference types in the group that participates after you. You will be given the advice receiver's preference over the objects and asked to give suggested ranking and verbal reasoning to support your advice. Please keep in mind that these preference types are hypothetical; in other words, they may or may not actually exist in the experiment. You will be given preferences different from that of yours, so please note that when you give advice to a particular type of subject, you must put yourself in their position to give advice. Do not simply repeat what you did in the experiment. Consider what you would have done if you were of that particular preference type. A typical advice giving page looks like the following figure.

As you can see, at the top of the page, your preference and priority will be repeated. Then your advisee's preferences over the objects and priorities will be provided. And your task is to choose what ranking you suggested him or her to take and provide verbal reasoning for it. Your reasoning should be concise and pertinent.

Finally, your payoff from advice giving is 1/2 of the payoff of the subject who receives your advice. If multiple subjects receive your advice, your payoff will be determined by randomly selecting one from such subjects. Therefore, you should provide advice to all your advisees to the best of your ability.
